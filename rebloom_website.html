Rooted in the field

We built the curriculum we couldn't find.

re:Bloom was built by people who have spent years inside licensed child care programs — observing classrooms, supporting directors, and watching dedicated educators struggle not because they lacked skill, but because the tools available to them were designed for someone else. Too expensive. Too complicated. Not built for the pace, the licensing demands, or the reality of a room with eight toddlers and one teacher.

We believe what happens in the first five years of a child's life is not preparation for learning. It is learning — the kind that shapes everything that follows. That conviction is not a marketing position. It is the reason every standard, every strategy, and every lesson plan in re:Bloom is grounded in decades of developmental research.

And we believe that access to high-quality curriculum should not be a function of a program's budget. A small family child care home and a large center-based program serve the same children and deserve the same tools. re:Bloom is priced to make that possible.

"The first five years shape the next eighty."

The principle that drives everything we build.

Deep field experience. Built by practitioners who have worked inside licensed child care at the classroom, program, and systems level.

Research-grounded, not trend-driven. Every domain, every strategy, every SoR recommendation traces back to peer-reviewed developmental science.

Priced for access, not prestige. Two plans at two price points — one for center-based programs, one built for the reality of a home-based business. No per-seat fees. No feature locks.

Four Curriculum Tracks

Built for every stage and every setting

Whether you run a center-based program or a family child care home, re:Bloom has a track built specifically for your setting — all nine domains woven throughout every plan.

Birth – 12 Months

Infant

Every caregiving routine is a learning opportunity. The infant track embeds all nine domains into feeding, floor play, and shared reading — from day one.

  • Serve-and-return language strategies
  • Safe sleep and licensing compliance built in
  • Sensory and cause-and-effect science
  • Attachment theory grounded

12 – 36 Months

Toddler

Toddlers need autonomy, language, and co-regulation. This track honors the "I do it myself" drive while building vocabulary, phonological awareness, and social skills.

  • Positive guidance and behavior support section
  • Syllable and rhyme awareness sequence
  • Autonomy-supportive practice strategies
  • Transition and routine planning tools

Ages 3 – 5

Preschool

The most literacy-intensive track, with a full Science of Reading sequence, kindergarten readiness alignment, and executive function development woven throughout.

  • Full SoR phonological awareness sequence
  • Kindergarten Readiness Alignment table
  • Tier 2 vocabulary instruction built in
  • Inquiry-based project learning model

Birth – Age 5 · Mixed-Age

Family Child Care

The only track built specifically for home-based, mixed-age settings. Every activity is designed so infants, toddlers, and preschoolers participate simultaneously — each at their own level.

  • Birth-through-5 continuum in one document
  • Mixed-age grouping strategies throughout
  • Home environment and FCC routine guidance
  • AI lesson plans that address all ages at once
